We’re on
Lord Street


Southport’s famous shopping boulevard
that inspired the design of modern Paris

• It's a little known fact that Napoléon III  (born Charles-Louis Napoleon Bonaparte) lived on Lord Street from May 1846, before becoming Emperor of France in 1851.

• Just a year later – no doubt inspired by his stay in the town – he set his Prefect of the Seine, Baron George Haussman, to work redesigning the city of Paris.

• Much of the medieval centre of Paris was replaced with broad tree-lined boulevards, covered walkways and arcades, just like Southport’s stylish Lord Street.
